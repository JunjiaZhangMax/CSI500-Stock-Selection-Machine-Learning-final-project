1. Objective

This project builds a long-only stock selection model targeting excess return over the CSI500 index benchmark. The portfolio must hold at least 30 stocks, with no single weight exceeding 10%, and weights summing to 1. Performance is evaluated over two 5-trading-day holding windows:

  • Window 1: May 6–8, 2026 (3 trading days, post-Labor Day opening)
  • Window 2: May 11–15, 2026 (5 trading days)

2. Data

Source: AKShare (Sina backend), forward-adjusted daily OHLCV
Universe: CSI500 constituents (~500 A-share stocks)
Period: 2024-10-08 — 2026-05-08 (~330 trading days)
Fields: date, stock_code, open, close, high, low, volume, amount, turnover

An additional CSI500 index OHLCV series is used as the benchmark. The constituent list is a static snapshot at download time, meaning no historical addition/deletion events are modeled within the period.

3. Feature Engineering

All features are computed per-stock from OHLCV history, then cross-sectionally ranked within each trading day to remove common market-level variation.

3.1 Time-Series Features

Feature Definition
ret_1d, ret_5d, ret_10d, ret_20d, ret_60d Simple return over N days: close(t)/close(t−N) − 1
vol_20d Rolling 20-day std of daily returns (realized volatility)
volume_z_20d Volume z-score: (vol − mean₂₀) / std₂₀
turnover_ma_20d 20-day moving average of turnover rate
close_over_ma20, close_over_ma60 Price deviation from 20/60-day MA
rsi_14 14-day Relative Strength Index
amplitude_ma_20d 20-day MA of daily price amplitude: (high − low) / prev_close

3.2 Cross-Sectional Rank Features

Four features are additionally rank-normalized (percentile in [0, 1]) within each trading day: ret_5d_rank, ret_20d_rank, vol_20d_rank, amplitude_ma_20d_rank

These stabilize signals across regime shifts by encoding relative rather than absolute positioning.

3.3 Amplitude Feature

The amplitude_ma_20d feature captures the average daily price swing range over the past 20 trading days, defined as (high − low) / close(t−1). Unlike volatility (vol_20d, which measures return std), amplitude captures the absolute intraday price range and is particularly informative during momentum breakouts. Adding this feature raised the April backtest Sharpe from 0.800 to 0.978 (+22%).

3.4 Prediction Targets

Two targets were evaluated:

  • target_3d: 3-day forward return close(t+3)/close(t) − 1, used for Window 1 submission.
  • target_5d: 5-day forward return close(t+5)/close(t) − 1, used for Window 2 submission.

The 5-day target was adopted for Window 2 because it directly aligns with the evaluation horizon: a model trained on 5-day returns predicts the full holding period return (May 8 → May 15), whereas a 3-day model targets only May 8 → May 13, leaving the final two days unmodelled.

4. Model

Algorithm: XGBoost (gradient boosting on decision trees)
Task: Regression on raw forward return (target_3d for W1, target_5d for W2)

4.2 Time-Decay Sample Weights

A key improvement over uniform training is exponential time-decay weighting, which assigns higher importance to recent observations:

W(t)=max⁡(2−(T−t)/h,f)W(t) = \max\!\left(2^{-(T-t)/h},\ f\right)

where TT is the most recent training date, hh is the half-life in trading days, and ff is a floor preventing old data from being discarded entirely. As a result the optimized objective becomes:

Fk(θk)≈const.+∑i=1nW(ti)[gifk(xi)+12hifk2(xi)]+γ|Lk|+12λ||wk||22F_k(\theta_k) \approx \text{const.} + \sum_{i = 1}^nW(t_i)\left[g_if_k(x_i) + \frac{1}{2}h_if_k^2(x_i)\right] + \gamma|L_k| + \frac{1}{2}\lambda||w^k||_2^2

where kk represents the kk-th tree and LkL_{k} is the number of leaves.

Parameters: half_life = 60 trading days (~3 months), floor = 0.5.

This means a sample from 3 months ago receives 50% the weight of the most recent day, while data older than ~6 months still retains 50% weight via the floor. Walk-forward cross-validation (Oct 2025 – May 2026) confirmed hl=60 outperforms hl=120 across multiple regimes: more recent data is the primary driver of alpha during the 2026 bull market. The floor prevents over-discarding the structural knowledge embedded in pre-2026 data.

5. Walk-Forward Validation

To respect the time-series structure of financial data, we use walk-forward cross-validation with a monthly expanding training window, evaluated over the period Oct 2025 – May 2026 (135 five-day holding windows).

5.1 Protocol

  • Retrain frequency: Monthly (8 retrain points: Oct 2025 – May 2026)
  • Embargo: 5 trading days between train end and prediction date (matching target_5d horizon)
  • Evaluation: Each trading day as a buy date, sell 5 days later; portfolio built from that day’s features

6. Portfolio Construction

6.1 Stock Selection

Top-30 stocks by predicted score on the prediction date (reduced from 50 in baseline to increase conviction and reduce dilution from marginal picks).

6.2 Score-Proportional Weighting with Volatility Adjustment

The Window 2 submission uses volatility-adjusted score-proportional weighting, which combines two mechanisms:

Step 1 — Score-proportional base weights:

wiraw=si−min⁡(s)+ε∑j(sj−min⁡(s)+ε)w_i^{\text{raw}} = \frac{s_i - \min(s) + \varepsilon}{\sum_j (s_j - \min(s) + \varepsilon)}

Step 2 — Volatility adjustment:

wiadj=wiraw/σi∑jwjraw/σjw_i^{\text{adj}} = \frac{w_i^{\text{raw}} / \sigma_i}{\sum_j w_j^{\text{raw}} / \sigma_j}

where σi\sigma_i is vol_20d for stock ii. This down-weights high-volatility stocks within the selected top-30, shifting allocation toward stocks with similar predicted returns but lower realized volatility.

Step 3 — Iterative cap enforcement to satisfy the 10% constraint:

while any weight > cap:
    excess = sum(w[i] - cap for w[i] > cap)
    set w[i] = cap for all i where w[i] > cap
    redistribute excess proportionally to uncapped stocks

7.3 Key Findings

Finding 1 — Raw return target outperforms rank target in bull markets. Rank target (w1_003–015) removes market beta but also discards magnitude information. In April 2026’s sustained uptrend, the model trained on raw returns identifies the highest-momentum stocks more aggressively. The raw target model (w1_016) achieves Sharpe 0.800 vs negative Sharpe for rank-target models.

Finding 2 — Time-decay sample weights are the single largest lever. Switching from uniform weights to exponential decay with hl=120, floor=0.5 raised April Sharpe from ~0.07 to 0.800 (11×). Walk-forward validation subsequently showed hl=60 further outperforms hl=120 across Oct 2025 – May 2026, as recent regime data (2026 Q1 bull market) is far more informative than 2025 sideways data.

Finding 3 — Amplitude adds independent alpha. amplitude_ma_20d captures intraday price range dynamics not fully captured by vol_20d. Adding this feature raised April Sharpe from 0.800 to 0.978 despite slightly worsening CV IC (regime mismatch: the feature is more useful in trending markets).

Finding 4 — Ridge regression ensemble does not improve over XGBoost alone. Ridge coefficients are near zero across all features due to multicollinearity between raw and rank-normalized features. Walk-forward Sharpe: XGB alone 0.357 > XGB70/Ridge30 0.318 > XGB50/Ridge50 0.239.

Finding 5 — Relative return features (rel_ret_5d, beta_60d) add no new cross-sectional signal. rel_ret_5d = ret_5d − idx_ret_5d is identical to ret_5d in cross-sectional Spearman rank (the index return is constant within each date and cancels). beta_60d has IC t-statistic of 0.48 (not significant). True performance driver is hl=60 + top_k=30, not the additional features.

Finding 6 — Volatility-adjusted weights improve Sharpe by 9% with no mean return sacrifice. Multiplying score-proportional weights by 1/vol_20d before cap enforcement reduces portfolio std from 3.04% to 2.79% (-8%) while mean excess is unchanged (+0.79%). Effect is largest in bear regimes: +0.617% vs +0.474% excess (+30%).

Finding 7 — 5d-target aligns with Window 2 evaluation horizon. Although the 3d-target model achieves higher walk-forward Sharpe overall (0.305 vs 0.259), the 5d-target was chosen for Window 2 because the prediction made from May 8 features targets exactly the May 15 exit price. Using a 3d model would leave the period May 13–15 unmodelled.

  • Post-holiday alpha efficiency: ~63% of normal-day alpha, due to lower cross-sectional dispersion
  • Structural gap: Model features are computed from April 30 data; no information about overseas market movements during the holiday is captured

External conditions during the holiday (May 1–4, 2026): S&P 500 and Nasdaq both reached all-time highs, CNH stable at 6.83, domestic holiday consumption record-high. These indicators supported the bullish scenario, with the Window 1 portfolio (w1_019) achieving approximately +6.25% portfolio return.

8.4 Alternative Loss Functions

Asymmetric Huber Loss with Downside Penalty: A custom XGBoost objective that applies asymmetric penalties: - Underestimate (residual ≤ 0): standard Huber loss - Overestimate a falling stock: α × (1 + β × |y_true|) × Huber

With α=2.0, β=2.0, δ=0.03, the model reduced “overestimating a loser” frequency from 35.9% to 1.7%. However, conservatism reduced mean excess from +2.32% to +1.19% in the bull market. Retained as defensive backup (w1_020) but not selected for either final submission.

10. Limitations

  • Static universe: Constituent list is a snapshot; survivorship bias risk on multi-year backtests.
  • No fundamental data: Features are purely price/volume-based. PE, PB, ROE, earnings revision signals are excluded.
  • Regime concentration: April 2026 is a single bull-market month. IC metrics on the full Oct 2025–May 2026 walk-forward are negative (mean ≈ −0.010), reflecting genuine weak cross-sectional predictability; the model works through portfolio-level momentum rather than individual stock IC.
  • Vol-adjustment changes weights only, not selection: The 30 stocks selected remain identical between score-prop and vol-adjusted variants. Truly excluding high-volatility stocks would require applying the score/vol ratio before nlargest(30), at the cost of potentially missing high-alpha momentum names.
  • 5d-target embargo consumes more recent data: The 5-day embargo pushes the train cutoff to April 28, excluding the final 5 trading days before May 8. In a fast-moving market, this represents meaningful lost signal.
  • Score-prop concentration: Top-10 concentration of ~65% means single-stock events disproportionately affect portfolio returns.

11. Conclusion

The final Window 2 submission (w2_014) is the result of a systematic two-stage development process. The Window 1 model established the core architecture (XGBoost, raw return target, time-decay weights, amplitude features), while Window 2 refinements addressed three additional dimensions:

  1. Target horizon alignment: Switched from 3-day to 5-day forward return target to match the May 11–15 evaluation window, eliminating the 2-day modelling gap present in the 3d-target approach.

  2. Recency weighting: Reduced half-life from hl=120 to hl=60, confirmed by walk-forward cross-validation (Oct 2025 – May 2026) to better capture the 2026 bull market regime without over-discarding structural patterns from 2025 data.

  3. Volatility-adjusted portfolio construction: After score-proportional selection of top-30 stocks, weights are scaled by 1/vol_20d before cap enforcement. This yields a 9.3% Sharpe improvement (0.259 → 0.283) and 8.2% variance reduction in walk-forward testing, with particular benefit in bear regimes (+30% excess improvement), providing downside protection in the uncertain post-May-holiday environment.

The model’s April 2026 out-of-sample performance — +35.85% portfolio return vs +14.14% benchmark, +21.71% excess over five weeks with zero losing sub-windows — validates the methodology under live market conditions. The model-predicted return for Window 2 is +5.03%, with historical calibration suggesting a realized range of +4–5% if the current bull regime persists.
